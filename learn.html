When does a habit become a problem?

This page explains the two main areas CTRL focuses on: gambling habits and social media habits.

Gambling warning signs

  • Spending more money than planned
  • Chasing losses to try to win money back
  • Hiding bets or lying about gambling
  • Feeling stressed, guilty, or anxious after betting
  • Betting more because sports content or ads keep pulling you back in

Social media warning signs

  • Losing track of time while scrolling
  • Feeling anxious when you cannot check your phone
  • Comparing your life to people online
  • Using scrolling to avoid stress or emotions
  • Seeing harmful content repeatedly because of algorithms

Research themes

The ideas guiding the website.

These themes help explain why CTRL focuses on early awareness instead of only giving help after the problem is already serious.

Lack of self-awareness

People may not realize their behavior is becoming unhealthy until it starts creating real consequences.

Digital reinforcement

Apps, feeds, and ads can keep pushing content that makes certain habits harder to step away from.

Fear of judgment

Students may avoid asking for help because they do not want to be embarrassed, labeled, or misunderstood.

Why students relate to this

It does not always feel like addiction at first.

A lot of the early signs can look like normal college behavior. That is why CTRL focuses on recognizing patterns before they become harder to break.

Example user scenario

A student starts placing small sports bets with friends during games. At first, it feels social and harmless. Over time, they start checking odds more often, chasing losses, and getting gambling content pushed to them online. CTRL is designed to help that student notice the pattern earlier.
